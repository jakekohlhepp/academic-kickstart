## Why it matters

This chapter is useful because the labor-supply elasticity debate is partly a translation problem. Micro evidence is often local, heterogeneous, and institution-specific, while macro models need aggregate objects that move equilibrium allocations.

## How to use it in this wiki

- Read it as background for micro-macro reconciliation papers by Rogerson-Wallenius, Keane-Rogerson, and Chetty.
- Use it when asking whether a micro estimate can discipline a macro calibration without losing heterogeneity and equilibrium feedback.
