## Why it matters

If you want a starting point for the modern elasticity debate, this is one of the cleanest places to begin. It is brief, but it names the dispute in a way the later literature keeps using.

## Short summary

Chetty, Guren, Manoli, and Weber review micro evidence and macro models through the lens of two margins: hours conditional on work, and whether to work at all. Their central claim is that modern micro estimates can be broadly consistent with macro steady-state elasticities on both margins, but they do not support the very large Frisch elasticities often used to explain business-cycle swings in aggregate hours.

In other words, the paper narrows part of the conflict and sharpens another part. The remaining puzzle is not simply "micro versus macro." It is especially about why the extensive-margin Frisch elasticity needed in some macro models is so large relative to the micro evidence.

## What to carry forward

- Separate intensive and extensive margins early.
- Be careful about which elasticity concept a paper is actually estimating.
- Read later Chetty papers as attempts to explain why observed micro responses can still coexist with larger aggregate responses.

## Caution

This is a review article, not a new estimation paper. Its value is conceptual compression and framing.
