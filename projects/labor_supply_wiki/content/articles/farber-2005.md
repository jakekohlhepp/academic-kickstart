## Why it matters

Farber shows that the taxi-driver debate is as much about measurement and decision timing as it is about behavioral preferences. A daily hours-wage regression compresses a sequence of trip and stopping decisions into two endogenous daily averages.

## What the paper does

The paper models a driver's labor supply as an intertemporal stopping problem. With a transitory change in earnings opportunities, wealth effects should be small. Under a standard model, cumulative hours should therefore be much more important than cumulative income in determining when the driver stops.

Using new NYC taxi data, Farber estimates the probability of ending a shift while controlling for clock time, day of week, weather, and location. Cumulative hours strongly predict stopping. Cumulative income is weakly related at best.

## What this changes

The result directly challenges the income-target interpretation of [Camerer et al.](../articles/camerer-babcock-loewenstein-thaler-1997-nyc-cabdrivers.html). Farber attributes the earlier negative elasticity to differences in empirical method and problems defining a daily wage when realized earnings per hour and stopping are jointly determined.

The deeper lesson is identification-specific: a negative relationship between daily hours and realized hourly earnings need not reveal a backward-bending labor-supply curve. It can arise because a driver who gets good fares reaches a given hours level with higher realized earnings, or because the wage measure incorporates the same choices it is meant to explain.

## Place in the continuing debate

Farber does not simply reject behavioral models and move on. [Farber (2008)](../articles/farber-2008-reference-dependent-preferences-taxi-drivers.html) estimates a reference-dependent model directly. [Farber (2015)](../articles/farber-2015-taxi-rain-labor-supply-lessons.html) revisits the question with the complete trip record and driver-level heterogeneity. The sequence is valuable because the question stays fixed while the data and estimand improve.

## Caution

The stopping model improves the alignment between theory and decision timing, but controls for earnings opportunities are still constructed rather than randomized. [Fehr and Goette](../articles/fehr-goette-2007-workers-work-more-high-wages.html) remain the cleaner experimental complement.
