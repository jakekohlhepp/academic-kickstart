## Why it matters

If Blinder and Weiss give the dynamic intuition, MaCurdy is one of the classic papers trying to estimate that dynamic logic from data. This is a major reference point for the life-cycle and Frisch-elasticity tradition.

## Short summary

MaCurdy uses longitudinal data to separate responses to temporary wage variation from responses tied to permanent lifetime resources. The paper is especially important for the claim that labor-supply elasticities depend on which concept of wage variation is doing the identifying.

In plain language, this paper helps explain why economists started distinguishing among Marshallian, Hicksian, and Frisch objects instead of talking about "the" labor-supply elasticity as if it were a single number.

## What to carry forward

- Identification matters as much as theory.
- Intertemporal substitution is not the same thing as long-run labor-supply response.
- The life-cycle setup becomes a backbone for later micro evidence and for later disputes with macro calibrations.

## Caution

The framework is tightly modeled and comes from a period when much of the empirical focus was on prime-age men. Later literatures broaden the margins, populations, and institutional settings.
