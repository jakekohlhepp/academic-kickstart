## The basic idea

If work builds human capital, then current hours affect future wages. That makes the labor-supply problem dynamic even before taxes, family choices, or macro aggregation enter.

## Reading path

Start with [Life-Cycle Labor Supply with Human Capital Accumulation](../articles/shaw-1989-life-cycle-labor-supply-human-capital.html) and [Human Capital and Labor Supply: A Synthesis](../articles/blinder-weiss-1976-human-capital-and-labor-supply.html). Then move to [Intertemporal Labor Supply and Human Capital Accumulation](../articles/imai-keane-2004-intertemporal-labor-supply-human-capital.html), [Human Capital Accumulation and the Intertemporal Elasticity of Substitution of Labor](../articles/wallenius-2011-human-capital-bias-ies-labor.html), and [Labour Supply: The Roles of Human Capital and the Extensive Margin](../articles/keane-wasi-2016-human-capital-extensive-margin.html).

## Why it changes elasticity interpretation

An observed response to wages may mix substitution, career investment, and future wage effects. That is why human-capital papers belong next to Frisch-elasticity and tax-policy papers.
