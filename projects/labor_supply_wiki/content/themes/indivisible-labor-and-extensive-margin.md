## Why this page matters

Many macro labor-supply elasticities are large because they rely on movement into and out of work, not because every worker smoothly changes weekly hours.

## Reading path

[Indivisible Labor, Lotteries, and Equilibrium](../articles/rogerson-1988-indivisible-labor-lotteries-equilibrium.html) is the starting point. [Does Indivisible Labor Explain the Difference between Micro and Macro Elasticities?](../articles/chetty-guren-manoli-weber-2013-indivisible-labor-meta-analysis.html) gives the empirical extensive-margin checkpoint. [The Anatomy of the Aggregate Labor Supply Elasticity](../articles/fiorito-zanella-2012-anatomy-aggregate-labor-supply-elasticity.html), [Reconciling Micro and Macro Estimates of the Frisch Labor Supply Elasticity](../articles/peterman-2016-reconciling-micro-macro-frisch-elasticity.html), and [Towards a Micro-Founded Theory of Aggregate Labour Supply](../articles/erosa-fuster-kambourov-2016-micro-founded-aggregate-labour-supply.html) show how this enters macro measurement.

## Link to scheduling

Indivisible labor is the macro cousin of the hours-constraints and discrete-scheduling theme. Both remind us that workers often choose among packages, not a smooth hours dial.
