## What ties these papers together

These papers put labor supply inside a dynamic household or life-cycle problem. The worker carries state variables forward: health, assets, wage risk, experience, family composition, or retirement status.

## Reading path

Use [Estimating Life Cycle Labor Supply Tax Effects](../articles/ziliak-kniesner-1999-life-cycle-labor-supply-tax-effects.html) and [Anticipated and Unanticipated Wage Changes, Wage Risk, and Intertemporal Labor Supply](../articles/pistaferri-2003-wage-risk-intertemporal-labor-supply.html) for the wage and tax side. Use [The Effects of Health, Wealth, and Wages on Labour Supply and Retirement Behaviour](../articles/french-2005-health-wealth-wages-retirement-labor-supply.html) for retirement. Use [Consumption Inequality and Family Labor Supply](../articles/blundell-pistaferri-saporta-eksten-2016-consumption-inequality-family-labor-supply.html) and [Consumption and Labor Supply with Partial Insurance](../articles/heathcote-storesletten-violante-2014-consumption-labor-supply-partial-insurance.html) for insurance and household responses.

## Why it matters

Structural life-cycle papers are often less transparent than reduced-form designs, but they are indispensable when the counterfactual changes future states, not only today's wage.
