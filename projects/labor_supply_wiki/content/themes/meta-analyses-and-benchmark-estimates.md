## Why this page matters

No single labor-supply estimate should carry the whole field. Meta-analyses and review papers help show how estimates vary by population, design, margin, horizon, and publication selection.

## Reading path

Use [Intertemporal Substitution in Labor Supply: A Meta-Analysis](../articles/elminejad-havranek-horvath-irsova-2023-intertemporal-substitution-meta-analysis.html) for Frisch/intertemporal substitution. Use [Review of Estimates of the Frisch Elasticity of Labor Supply](../articles/whalen-reichling-2017-cbo-review-frisch-elasticity.html) for applied calibration. Use [Does Indivisible Labor Explain the Difference between Micro and Macro Elasticities?](../articles/chetty-guren-manoli-weber-2013-indivisible-labor-meta-analysis.html) for extensive-margin meta-analysis.

## Practical use

This page is where to go when a model needs a defensible range rather than a favorite point estimate.
