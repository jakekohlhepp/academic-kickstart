## Why this page exists

Platform work gives researchers high-frequency wage, demand, and participation variation. It is therefore a modern laboratory for labor supply, but one where market design and platform equilibrium matter.

## Reading path

Start with [An Analysis of the Labor Market for Uber's Driver-Partners in the United States](../articles/hall-krueger-2018-uber-driver-partners-labor-market.html), then read [The Value of Flexible Work](../articles/chen-chevalier-rossi-oehlsen-2019-value-of-flexible-work.html). Use [Dynamic Pricing in a Labor Market](../articles/chen-sheldon-2015-surge-pricing-flexible-work-uber.html), [Uber versus Taxi](../articles/angrist-caldwell-hall-2021-uber-versus-taxi.html), and [Ride-Sharing Markets Re-Equilibrate](../articles/hall-horton-knoepfle-2023-ridesharing-markets-reequilibrate.html) for pricing, comparison, and equilibrium.

## Link to taxi drivers

The taxi-driver literature is the older cousin of this theme. It asks similar questions about temporary wage variation and flexible daily labor supply.
