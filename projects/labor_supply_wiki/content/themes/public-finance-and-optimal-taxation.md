## The public-finance use case

Public finance often needs a tractable elasticity for welfare calculations or optimal tax formulas. The hard part is knowing which empirical object is legitimate for that use.

## Reading path

[Using Elasticities to Derive Optimal Income Tax Rates](../articles/saez-2001-elasticities-optimal-income-tax-rates.html) is the formula anchor. [Optimal Redistributive Taxation with Both Extensive and Intensive Responses](../articles/jacquet-lehmann-van-der-linden-2013-optimal-tax-heterogeneous-elasticities.html) adds heterogeneity and margins. [Review of Estimates of the Frisch Elasticity of Labor Supply](../articles/whalen-reichling-2017-cbo-review-frisch-elasticity.html) is a practical calibration reference.

## Link to ETI

The taxable-income literature is nearby because ETI can be a sufficient statistic under some conditions. The conditions matter.
