## What this page is for

This page is for the moment when the question is no longer "what is a Frisch elasticity?" but "how much should I trust the estimate?"

It sits downstream from the older Frisch-foundations page. Once the object is defined, the next problem is how much uncertainty remains because of measurement error, functional form, wage risk, participation selection, and publication or design heterogeneity.

## The compact reading path

[Robust Inference for the Frisch Labor Supply Elasticity](../articles/keane-neal-2025-robust-inference-frisch-labor-supply-elasticity.html) is the direct methods paper. [Intertemporal Substitution in Labor Supply: A Meta-Analysis](../articles/elminejad-havranek-horvath-irsova-2023-intertemporal-substitution-meta-analysis.html) and [Review of Estimates of the Frisch Elasticity of Labor Supply](../articles/whalen-reichling-2017-cbo-review-frisch-elasticity.html) provide benchmarking. [On the Measurement of the Elasticity of Labour](../articles/gottlieb-onken-2021-measurement-elasticity-labour.html) keeps the measurement object visible.

## What "robust" should mean here

Robustness is not only a wider standard error. It should mean that the estimate survives reasonable changes in the wage process, the treatment of participation, the timing of labor supply, and the state variables held fixed. A Frisch estimate that is precise only under a narrow maintained structure is still a fragile input for macro calibration.

## How to use this page

Use this page as a checkpoint before borrowing a Frisch number. The benchmark question is: does the paper identify intertemporal substitution holding wealth constant, or is it closer to an observed hours response that also reflects income effects, human capital, fixed costs, or selection?

## Practical rule

Report the elasticity object, the identifying variation, and the uncertainty together. A precise number without the first two is not very informative.
