## Why this theme matters

Much of the labor-supply literature becomes concrete only when it is attached to taxes and benefits. Policy creates wedges, nonlinear budget sets, participation penalties, and incentives to change hours.

## A useful way to organize the papers

- [Family Labor Supply with Taxes](../articles/hausman-ruud-1984-family-labor-supply-with-taxes.html) is the classic household-tax starting point.
- [Are Micro and Macro Labor Supply Elasticities Consistent?](../articles/chetty-guren-manoli-weber-2011-micro-vs-macro-elasticities.html) tells you which elasticity concepts matter for policy.
- [Adjustment Costs, Firm Responses, and Micro vs. Macro Labor Supply Elasticities](../articles/chetty-friedman-olsen-pistaferri-2011-adjustment-costs.html) shows why workers may not move smoothly when policy changes.
- [Bounds on Elasticities With Optimization Frictions](../articles/chetty-2012-bounds-on-elasticities.html) shows why welfare and measurement both change when those frictions are real.
- [Substitution and Income Effects of Labor Income Taxation](../articles/graber-havarstein-mogstad-torsvik-vestad-2026-substitution-income-effects-taxation.html) is the new bridge paper if you want ETI estimates translated back into substitution effects, income effects, and actual labor-supply elasticities.
- [The Elasticity of Taxable Income: Evidence and Implications](../articles/gruber-saez-2002-elasticity-of-taxable-income.html) is a core ETI benchmark when the question becomes how taxable income, not just hours, responds to tax reforms.
- [Work incentives at the extensive and intensive margin in Europe](../articles/jara-gasior-makovec-2020-work-incentives-europe.html) gives a newer cross-country policy map.
- [Own-Wage Labor Supply Elasticities](../articles/bargain-peichl-2016-own-wage-labor-supply-elasticities.html) helps you place newer estimates in the wider literature.

## The recurring lesson

Policy often works through different margins for different groups. Second earners, lower-income households, and workers facing lumpy job offers can all see very different incentive environments even under the same formal tax system.
