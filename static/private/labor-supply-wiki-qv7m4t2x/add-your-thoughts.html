Workflow

Add Your Thoughts

A low-friction workflow for turning free-flow notes into integrated wiki pages.

The intended workflow

  1. Keep writing however you like in Google Docs.
  2. When you want the wiki updated, paste the latest raw text into notes/inbox/google-doc-notes.md.
  3. Tell me to integrate it.

What I will do with those notes

  • Pull article-specific ideas into the right article pages.
  • Add cross-paper patterns to the relevant theme pages.
  • Spin out a new page if your notes are really a new theme.
  • Preserve raw language when it is useful, but rewrite for clarity when the wiki needs a cleaner voice.

If you would rather use a link

If your Google Doc is public or published to the web, you can give me that link instead of pasting text into the inbox file. I can use the published text as the source for an integration pass.

Good things to dump into the inbox

  • "This paper feels more about search frictions than pure hours choice."
  • "Put Hausman next to Jara because both are really about tax wedges."
  • "I keep forgetting the difference between Hicksian and Frisch in these pages."
  • "Make a future theme page on second earners and family labor supply."

What not to worry about

Do not worry about writing polished prose. The inbox is meant for rough thinking.

Built from local markdown, saved PDF copies, and source links. Open site/index.html on any device-friendly browser.
