Why it matters

This is one of the classic papers that turns hours mismatch into an equilibrium issue. If jobs offer tied hours-wage packages, then workers who want different hours may accept or leave jobs in ways that reveal how costly those mismatches are.

Short summary

Altonji and Paxson study the relationship between hours changes and wage changes when workers switch jobs. Their central idea is that workers facing unattractive hours will require wage compensation, and workers who move toward better hours may accept smaller wage gains. The evidence supports the idea that hours constraints and preferred-hours mismatch matter for job mobility and wage-hours trade-offs.

This helps move the literature away from the idea that workers simply choose their favorite number of hours within each job. Instead, they often choose among jobs that bundle wages and hours together.

What to carry forward

  • Hours mismatch can show up as compensating wage differentials.
  • Job mobility helps reveal hours constraints.
  • The relevant unit is often the job package, not an unconstrained hours choice.

Caution

The paper is conceptually strong but based on limited information about the magnitude of constraints. It is best read as an early empirical bridge between mismatch and job-package theory.
