Why it matters

Once you have read the Chetty cluster, this is a good calibration page. It surveys what the elasticity literature actually looks like across methods and samples instead of asking for a single favorite estimate.

Short summary

Bargain and Peichl review own-wage labor-supply estimates and emphasize that heterogeneity is a feature, not a bug. Prime-age men tend to show smaller responses than women and secondary earners, and extensive-margin responses often matter more than intensive-margin responses in policy-relevant settings.

The paper is also valuable because it makes clear that measurement strategy changes what gets estimated. Structural models, quasi-experimental designs, and cross-country comparisons can all be informative, but they need to be read as answers to somewhat different questions.

What to carry forward

  • Do not expect a single elasticity for all workers.
  • Extensive-margin response is often central for policy.
  • Method choice changes interpretation.

Caution

This is a review article. It is excellent for orientation, but the underlying studies remain heterogeneous in population, institution, and identification.
