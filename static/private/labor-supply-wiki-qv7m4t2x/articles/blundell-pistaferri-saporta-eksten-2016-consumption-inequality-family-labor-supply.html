Consumption Inequality and Family Labor Supply

A structural life-cycle paper linking family labor supply to consumption inequality and insurance.

Authors
Richard Blundell, Luigi Pistaferri, Itay Saporta-Eksten
Journal
American Economic Review
Year
2016

Related themes

life cycle structural estimationlife cycle and family labor supplyaggregation heterogeneous elasticities

Why it matters

This paper makes family labor supply part of the household insurance problem. It is useful for the wiki because labor supply is not only a response to wages or taxes; it is also a way households absorb shocks.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Read it with Heathcote-Storesletten-Violante on consumption and labor supply under incomplete insurance.
  • Use it when asking how spousal labor supply changes the mapping from wage shocks to consumption inequality.
