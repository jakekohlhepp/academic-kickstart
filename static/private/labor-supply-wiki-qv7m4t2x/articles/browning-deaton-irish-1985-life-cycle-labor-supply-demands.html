Econometrica - 1985

A Profitable Approach to Labor Supply and Commodity Demands over the Life-Cycle

A life-cycle demand-system paper that helps place labor supply inside a broader intertemporal allocation problem.

Authors
Martin Browning, Angus Deaton, Margaret Irish
Journal
Econometrica
Year
1985

Related themes

frisch elasticity foundationslife cycle and family labor supplyutility functions and dynamic vs static models

Why it matters

The paper is central for reading labor supply as one part of a larger life-cycle system. It connects consumption, commodity demands, and labor supply in a way that later Frisch-elasticity and dynamic-utility work keeps leaning on.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Pair it with MaCurdy (1981) and Altonji (1986) when tracking the early empirical Frisch-elasticity literature.
  • Use it on the model side, not only as an elasticity estimate: it clarifies what it means to condition on the marginal utility of wealth.
