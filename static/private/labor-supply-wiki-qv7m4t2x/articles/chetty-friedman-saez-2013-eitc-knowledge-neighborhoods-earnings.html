American Economic Review - 2013

Using Differences in Knowledge across Neighborhoods to Uncover the Impacts of the EITC on Earnings

An EITC paper showing how information and local knowledge shape earnings responses to tax incentives.

Authors
Raj Chetty, John N. Friedman, Emmanuel Saez
Journal
American Economic Review
Year
2013

Related themes

bunching tax policytaxes benefits and work incentivesestimation strategies and relative credibility

Why it matters

This paper connects tax incentives to knowledge frictions. Workers may respond differently not only because of preferences or constraints, but because they understand the schedule differently.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Read it with Chetty (2012) on optimization frictions.
  • Use it when policy salience and local information are part of the labor-supply response.
