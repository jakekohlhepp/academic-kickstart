Labor Supply with Hours Restrictions

Why it matters

This is one of the foundational structural papers in the hours-restrictions literature. It gives a concrete model of what it means for workers to face limited hours offers instead of a smooth hours margin.

Short summary

Dickens and Lundberg model labor supply as a discrete-choice problem in which workers draw from a finite set of employment opportunities, each with fixed hours. They estimate substantial differences between desired and actual hours and show that ignoring restrictions on offered hours can distort policy conclusions and elasticity estimates.

This paper matters because it makes hours mismatch endogenous to the offer distribution. The question is not just what workers want. It is what jobs are available.

What to carry forward

  • Hours restriction can be modeled as limited job offers.
  • The distribution of offered hours matters for policy analysis.
  • Structural models of constrained choice remain highly relevant for newer job-package and matching literatures.

Caution

This is a stylized structural model, so its usefulness depends on whether the job-offer framework is a good approximation in the setting you care about. But as a conceptual benchmark it is still very strong.
