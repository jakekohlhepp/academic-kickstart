Towards a Micro-Founded Theory of Aggregate Labour Supply

A life-cycle model with both intensive and extensive margins aimed at micro-founding aggregate labor supply.

Authors
Andres Erosa, Luisa Fuster, Gueorgui Kambourov
Journal
Review of Economic Studies
Year
2016

Related themes

indivisible labor and extensive marginmicro vs macro elasticitieslife cycle structural estimation

Why it matters

The paper directly addresses the problem of building aggregate labor supply from individual choices over the life cycle. It is a natural bridge between macro calibration and micro evidence.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Use it with Rogerson-Wallenius and Keane-Rogerson.
  • Keep track of which margin is moving: hours, participation, or the life-cycle timing of work.
