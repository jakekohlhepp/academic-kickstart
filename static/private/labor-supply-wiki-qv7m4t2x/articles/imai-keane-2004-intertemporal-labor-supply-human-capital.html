International Economic Review - 2004

Intertemporal Labor Supply and Human Capital Accumulation

A key paper arguing that human-capital accumulation can strongly affect estimated intertemporal labor-supply responses.

Authors
Susumu Imai, Michael P. Keane
Journal
International Economic Review
Year
2004

Related themes

human capital and labor supplylife cycle and family labor supplyutility functions and dynamic vs static models

Why it matters

The paper shifts attention from hours today to the fact that work today can raise wages tomorrow. Once work builds human capital, a wage response is not just a consumption-leisure tradeoff.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Read it with Shaw (1989), Wallenius (2011), and Keane-Wasi (2016).
  • Use it whenever a Frisch estimate may be biased because current work changes future productivity.
