Journal of Economic Literature - 2012

Micro and Macro Labor Supply Elasticities: A Reassessment of Conventional Wisdom

A major reassessment arguing that the micro evidence is more compatible with larger macro labor-supply elasticities than conventional wisdom suggests.

Authors
Michael P. Keane, Richard Rogerson
Journal
Journal of Economic Literature
Year
2012

Related themes

Why it matters

This paper is one of the core counterweights to readings of the micro literature that emphasize only small elasticities. It organizes the debate around which elasticity is being estimated, which margin moves, and how dynamic behavior enters.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Read it against Chetty (2012) and the Chetty-Guren-Manoli-Weber papers.
  • Use it as a guide to why life-cycle structure, human capital, and participation can change the policy-relevant elasticity.
