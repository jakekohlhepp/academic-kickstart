Annual Review of Economics - 2015

Reconciling Micro and Macro Labor Supply Elasticities: A Structural Perspective

A structural guide to why micro estimates and macro calibrations often appear to disagree.

Authors
Michael P. Keane, Richard Rogerson
Journal
Annual Review of Economics
Year
2015

Related themes

micro vs macro elasticitiesutility functions and dynamic vs static modelsestimation strategies and relative credibility

Why it matters

The paper is useful because it treats reconciliation as a modeling problem. Differences across studies can reflect timing, margins, fixed costs, human capital, and aggregation rather than a simple right-versus-wrong elasticity.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Use it after Keane and Rogerson (2012) for the structural version of the argument.
  • Pair it with Chetty (2012) to compare friction-based and structural-life-cycle reconciliations.
