Why it matters

This paper matters because it treats schedule control as an organizational variable rather than an individual perk. That is exactly the kind of mechanism standard labor-supply models often leave in the background.

Short summary

Kelly, Moen, and Tranby study the Results Only Work Environment initiative in a white-collar organization. The intervention increased employee control over time and reduced work-family conflict by weakening face-time norms and emphasizing output over physical presence. The broad lesson is that organizations can change the utility cost of working by changing time expectations and supervisory rules.

For the wiki, this is labor supply in a broad but important sense. A job can become easier or harder to sustain because the organization redesigns time, even if hourly pay does not move.

What to carry forward

  • Schedule control is an organizational design choice.
  • Face-time norms can act like hidden taxes on labor supply.
  • Work-family conflict is one channel through which organizations shape participation and retention.

Caution

This paper is less about estimating a conventional wage elasticity than about identifying a mechanism. It is most useful as a bridge between labor-supply economics and sociology of organizations.
