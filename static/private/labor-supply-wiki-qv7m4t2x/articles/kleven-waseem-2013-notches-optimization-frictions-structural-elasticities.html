Quarterly Journal of Economics - 2013

Using Notches to Uncover Optimization Frictions and Structural Elasticities

A benchmark bunching paper using tax notches to separate frictions from structural elasticities.

Authors
Henrik Kleven, Mazhar Waseem
Journal
Quarterly Journal of Economics
Year
2013

Related themes

bunching tax policytaxes benefits and work incentivesestimation strategies and relative credibility

Why it matters

Kleven and Waseem are central for the bunching literature because notches reveal both behavioral response and optimization frictions. The paper pairs naturally with Chetty's optimization-frictions framework.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Use it as the main notch-based complement to Chetty (2012).
  • Read it when a tax response is muted because adjustment is costly or inattentive, not because preferences are inelastic.
