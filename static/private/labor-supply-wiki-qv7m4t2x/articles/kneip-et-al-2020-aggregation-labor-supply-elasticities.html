Aggregation and Labor Supply Elasticities

A modern contribution on how heterogeneous labor-supply responses aggregate.

Why it matters

Kneip et al. belong in the wiki because aggregation is one of the main ways labor-supply estimates become policy objects. The paper helps frame what can go wrong when heterogeneous responses are compressed into a single number.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Read it with Attanasio et al. and Heathcote-Storesletten-Violante.
  • Use it as a methodological warning label whenever a theme page talks about aggregate elasticities.
