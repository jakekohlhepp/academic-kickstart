Danish tax reform evidence - 2016

Tax Reforms and Intertemporal Shifting of Wage Income

A Danish tax-reform paper useful for separating real labor-supply responses from timing or shifting responses.

Authors
Claus Thustrup Kreiner, Soren Leth-Petersen, Peer Ebbesen Skov
Journal
Danish tax reform evidence
Year

Related themes

Why it matters

The paper is important because tax elasticities can include timing and reporting behavior, not only changes in actual work effort. That distinction is central to the wiki's ETI theme.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Pair it with Kleven-Schultz and Chetty-Friedman-Olsen-Pistaferri.
  • Use it whenever a tax reform creates scope for intertemporal shifting.
