Why it matters

This paper is one of the closest methodological precursors to the new Graber paper. It pushes directly on the causal interpretation of ETI estimands when taxpayers are heterogeneous.

Short summary

Kumar and Liang show that commonly used instruments in the ETI literature can fail when elasticities vary across individuals. They develop alternative instruments and show empirically that heterogeneity can explain a substantial share of the divergence in earlier ETI estimates.

The paper is useful because it sharpens a methodological point that often gets blurred in applied tax work: if heterogeneity matters, then the estimator may not recover any policy-relevant average unless the design is set up very carefully. That is exactly the door Graber et al. walk through.

What to carry forward

  • Heterogeneity is an identification problem, not just a descriptive add-on.
  • Standard ETI instruments can be invalid or hard to interpret under heterogeneous responses.
  • Method papers matter because they change what older empirical estimates can be said to mean.

Caution

This is a technical paper. Its main contribution is about interpretation and estimator design, so it is best paired with a more empirical ETI paper rather than read alone.
