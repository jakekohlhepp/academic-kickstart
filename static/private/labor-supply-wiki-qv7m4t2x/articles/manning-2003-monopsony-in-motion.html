Monopsony in Motion

The modern monopsony book that reframes labor supply to firms as upward sloping because of search frictions and job differentiation.

Authors
Alan Manning
Journal
Princeton University Press

Related themes

Why it matters

Manning is the core reference for firm-side labor supply. It changes the question from "how many hours do workers choose?" to "how elastic is the labor supply curve facing a particular employer?"

How to use it in this wiki

  • Use it as the foundation for monopsony and firm wage-setting entries.
  • Pair it with job-amenity and organization pages when wages are only one part of the job package.
