Why it matters

This is the best overview page for the Mas-Pallais corner of the field. It takes the experimental findings on worker preferences and places them inside a wider literature on telework, scheduling, contracting, and work-life arrangements.

Short summary

Mas and Pallais review alternative work arrangements defined both by working conditions and by the worker's relationship to the employer. They synthesize what we know about worker preferences for these arrangements, firms' incentives to offer them, and the role of regulation. A key descriptive point is that jobs with location or schedule flexibility are not automatically the same as family-friendly jobs.

The review matters because it makes hours mismatch look broader than a simple hours gap. Mismatch can be about timing, predictability, remote feasibility, task structure, and the kind of employment relationship on offer.

What to carry forward

  • Work arrangements are multidimensional.
  • Worker preferences and firm offerings jointly shape observed labor supply.
  • Regulation can affect work arrangements by changing both firm behavior and worker surplus.

Caution

This is a review article, not a new identification design. Its value is synthesis and agenda-setting.
