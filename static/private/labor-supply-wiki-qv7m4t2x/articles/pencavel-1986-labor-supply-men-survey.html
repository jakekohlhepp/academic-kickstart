Why it matters

Pencavel is a benchmark for the pre-Chetty, pre-ETI labor-supply literature. It gives the older evidence base on male hours and wages that later surveys either build on or revise.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Use it as the older survey anchor before Blundell and MaCurdy (1999) and Keane (2011).
  • Keep it nearby when the wiki distinguishes male intensive-margin estimates from household, female, and extensive-margin responses.
