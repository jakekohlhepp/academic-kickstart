Why Do Americans Work So Much More Than Europeans?

A provocative macro-tax argument about cross-country differences in work hours.

Authors
Edward C. Prescott
Journal
Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis Quarterly Review
Year

Related themes

macro business cycle labor supplytaxes benefits and work incentivespublic finance and optimal taxation

Why it matters

Prescott is a key reference for the claim that tax wedges explain large cross-country differences in hours. It is also a useful foil for papers that emphasize institutions, participation, or model structure.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Pair it with Ljungqvist-Sargent and Blundell-Bozio-Laroque.
  • Use it when comparing tax-based macro explanations with micro elasticity evidence.
