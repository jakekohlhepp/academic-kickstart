Why it matters

This is the review page you want if you need the ETI literature explained at a high level without losing the theoretical stakes. It is the best single bridge between empirical tax responses and welfare analysis.

Short summary

Saez, Slemrod, and Giertz survey the growing ETI literature and give it a disciplined conceptual frame. They show when the ETI can be interpreted as a sufficient statistic for optimal tax and efficiency analysis, and when additional parameters are needed because avoidance, timing, base definitions, or income effects complicate the mapping from taxable income to welfare-relevant labor supply.

The review is useful because it is both theoretical and empirical. It explains the object, then uses major empirical studies to show why identification is difficult and why estimates vary across settings and tax bases.

What to carry forward

  • ETI is a powerful object only under specific conditions.
  • Institutional details of the tax base matter for interpretation.
  • Review articles can sharply improve how individual empirical papers are read.

Caution

Because the paper organizes a heterogeneous literature, it cannot settle every identification dispute. It is best used as a framework for judging individual ETI papers, not as a substitute for them.
