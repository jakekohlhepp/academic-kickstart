Review of Economic Dynamics - 2011

Human Capital Accumulation and the Intertemporal Elasticity of Substitution of Labor: How Large is the Bias?

A direct treatment of how human-capital accumulation biases estimates of intertemporal labor-supply substitution.

Authors
Johanna Wallenius
Journal
Review of Economic Dynamics
Year
2011

Related themes

human capital and labor supplyfrisch elasticity foundationsmicro vs macro elasticities

Why it matters

Wallenius makes the human-capital critique explicit: if hours raise future wages, standard intertemporal substitution estimates may not recover the clean Frisch object researchers want.

How to use it in this wiki

  • Use it as the methodological companion to Imai-Keane.
  • Keep it in view when comparing static tax responses with life-cycle elasticities.
