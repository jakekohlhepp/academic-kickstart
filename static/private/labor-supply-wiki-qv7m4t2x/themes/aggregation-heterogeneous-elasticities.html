Theme page

Aggregation of heterogeneous elasticities

Papers on why heterogeneous individual responses do not collapse into one policy-relevant elasticity by simple averaging.

The central problem

Labor-supply elasticities vary across people, margins, wages, institutions, and horizons. Aggregation is therefore an economic problem, not a spreadsheet operation.

Reading path

Start with What Has Been Learned About Labor Supply in the Past Twenty Years? and Micro Data and General Equilibrium Models. Then use Aggregating Elasticities, Aggregation and Labor Supply Elasticities, and Labour Supply Responses and the Extensive Margin for modern applications.

Why it matters

Aggregation is where a credible local estimate can still become the wrong policy input. The population weights, margins, and institutions matter.
