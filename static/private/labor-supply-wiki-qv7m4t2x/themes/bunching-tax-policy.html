Theme page

Bunching and tax-policy elasticities

Papers using notches, kinks, tax schedules, and policy salience to infer labor-supply and taxable-income responses.

Why this page exists

Bunching and tax-reform designs are attractive because policy schedules create sharp incentives. But these estimates often mix real labor-supply responses, reporting, timing, knowledge, and optimization frictions.

Reading path

Start with Bounds on Elasticities With Optimization Frictions, then read Using Notches to Uncover Optimization Frictions and Structural Elasticities. Use Using Differences in Knowledge across Neighborhoods to Uncover the Impacts of the EITC on Earnings for salience and knowledge, and Tax Reforms and Intertemporal Shifting of Wage Income for timing responses.

Practical warning

A sharp policy design can identify a sharp local response while still leaving open whether the response is hours, effort, reporting, or timing.
