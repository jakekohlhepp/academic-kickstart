Theme page

Frisch elasticity foundations

The core life-cycle, intertemporal-substitution, and Frisch-elasticity papers behind the modern labor-supply debate.

Why this page exists

The Frisch elasticity is one of the most reused and most contested objects in labor supply. This page gathers the older empirical foundations, the survey anchors, and the newer inference and meta-analysis work.

Reading path

  1. Start with An Empirical Model of Labor Supply in a Life-Cycle Setting for the classic panel-data life-cycle estimate.
  2. Read A Profitable Approach to Labor Supply and Commodity Demands over the Life-Cycle and Intertemporal Substitution in Labor Supply for the broader intertemporal-substitution foundation.
  3. Use Labor Supply of Men: A Survey, Labor Supply: A Review of Alternative Approaches, and Micro Data and General Equilibrium Models to keep the evidence and aggregation issues straight.
  4. Use Review of Estimates of the Frisch Elasticity of Labor Supply, Intertemporal Substitution in Labor Supply: A Meta-Analysis, and Robust Inference for the Frisch Labor Supply Elasticity for modern benchmarking and uncertainty.

Core warning

The same word "elasticity" can refer to short-run hours adjustment, intertemporal substitution holding wealth constant, lifetime labor-supply reallocation, or an aggregate macro object. Most disagreements start there.

Connection map

Use human capital and labor supply when a Frisch estimate might be contaminated by returns to experience. Use aggregation of heterogeneous elasticities when the object is a population average rather than an individual parameter. Use macro and business-cycle labor supply when the elasticity is doing work inside a calibrated aggregate model.

The practical sequence is therefore: define the elasticity object, ask what state variables are being held fixed, then ask whether the estimate is being used for individual behavior, policy counterfactuals, or macro calibration.
