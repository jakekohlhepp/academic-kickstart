Theme page

Indivisible labor and extensive-margin models

Models and evidence where aggregate labor supply moves through employment, participation, or fixed work packages rather than smooth hours.

Why this page matters

Many macro labor-supply elasticities are large because they rely on movement into and out of work, not because every worker smoothly changes weekly hours.

Reading path

Indivisible Labor, Lotteries, and Equilibrium is the starting point. Does Indivisible Labor Explain the Difference between Micro and Macro Elasticities? gives the empirical extensive-margin checkpoint. The Anatomy of the Aggregate Labor Supply Elasticity, Reconciling Micro and Macro Estimates of the Frisch Labor Supply Elasticity, and Towards a Micro-Founded Theory of Aggregate Labour Supply show how this enters macro measurement.

Link to scheduling

Indivisible labor is the macro cousin of the hours-constraints and discrete-scheduling theme. Both remind us that workers often choose among packages, not a smooth hours dial.
