Theme page

Life-cycle structural estimation

Dynamic structural papers where labor supply interacts with taxes, risk, health, wealth, retirement, and family insurance.

What ties these papers together

These papers put labor supply inside a dynamic household or life-cycle problem. The worker carries state variables forward: health, assets, wage risk, experience, family composition, or retirement status.

Reading path

Use Estimating Life Cycle Labor Supply Tax Effects and Anticipated and Unanticipated Wage Changes, Wage Risk, and Intertemporal Labor Supply for the wage and tax side. Use The Effects of Health, Wealth, and Wages on Labour Supply and Retirement Behaviour for retirement. Use Consumption Inequality and Family Labor Supply and Consumption and Labor Supply with Partial Insurance for insurance and household responses.

Why it matters

Structural life-cycle papers are often less transparent than reduced-form designs, but they are indispensable when the counterfactual changes future states, not only today's wage.
