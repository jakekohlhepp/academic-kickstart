Theme page

Micro vs. macro labor supply elasticities

A guide to the modern elasticity debate, with Chetty-era papers as the core reading path.

The basic question

Why do many micro studies find modest labor-supply responses while macro models sometimes need much larger elasticities? This theme page is the quickest way into that question.

Reading path

  1. Start with Are Micro and Macro Labor Supply Elasticities Consistent? A Review of Evidence on the Intensive and Extensive Margins for the clean statement of the puzzle.
  2. Move to Adjustment Costs, Firm Responses, and Micro vs. Macro Labor Supply Elasticities for one mechanism that can reconcile the two.
  3. Read Bounds on Elasticities With Optimization Frictions for the formal framework behind that reconciliation.
  4. Read Micro versus Macro Labor Supply Elasticities: The Role of Dynamic Returns to Effort for a recent frontier paper on delayed adjustment through job ladders and mobility.
  5. Use Own-Wage Labor Supply Elasticities as a later survey of what the empirical distribution of estimates actually looks like.

The expanded structural route runs through Micro and Macro Elasticities in a Life Cycle Model with Taxes, Micro and Macro Labor Supply Elasticities, and Reconciling Micro and Macro Labor Supply Elasticities. Use Does Indivisible Labor Explain the Difference between Micro and Macro Elasticities? when the extensive margin is the central mechanism.

The main lesson

The argument is no longer just "micro versus macro." The real issue is which margin is moving, over what horizon, under what frictions, and with what identifying variation.

Why the older literature still matters

An Empirical Model of Labor Supply in a Life-Cycle Setting matters here because the later elasticity debate still depends on distinctions the older life-cycle literature pushed hard: temporary versus permanent wage changes, intertemporal substitution, and the meaning of a Frisch object.
