Theme page

Monopsony and firm wage-setting

A firm-facing labor-supply theme where employers face upward-sloping supply because of search frictions, outside options, concentration, and job differentiation.

The basic shift

Monopsony changes the labor-supply question. Instead of only asking how workers choose hours at a market wage, it asks how many workers a specific firm can attract and retain at a given wage and job bundle.

Reading path

Start with Monopsony in Motion. Then use Firm Wage-Setting Power and Outside Options and Labor Market Concentration and Employer Wage-Setting Power for modern firm-side wage-setting references. Pair them with Compensating Differentials in Bundles of Job Attributes and Valuing Alternative Work Arrangements.

Why it connects to organizations

The firm-facing supply curve is shaped by scheduling, amenities, task assignment, outside options, and coordination costs. It is not only a wage curve.
