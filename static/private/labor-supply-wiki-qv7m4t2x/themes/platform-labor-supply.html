Theme page

Platform and ridesharing labor supply

Uber, ride-sharing, and platform-work papers where labor supply is flexible, high frequency, and embedded in a two-sided market.

Why this page exists

Platform work gives researchers high-frequency wage, demand, and participation variation. It is therefore a modern laboratory for labor supply, but one where market design and platform equilibrium matter.

Reading path

Start with An Analysis of the Labor Market for Uber's Driver-Partners in the United States, then read The Value of Flexible Work. Use Dynamic Pricing in a Labor Market, Uber versus Taxi, and Ride-Sharing Markets Re-Equilibrate for pricing, comparison, and equilibrium.

Link to taxi drivers

The taxi-driver literature is the older cousin of this theme. It asks similar questions about temporary wage variation and flexible daily labor supply.

Three bridges

First, platform work links to taxi driver labor supply through high-frequency wage variation and reference-dependence debates. Second, it links to hours mismatch and alternative work arrangements because platforms offer unusually fine schedule choice. Third, it links to monopsony and firm wage-setting because outside options and platform design shape the labor supply curve facing the platform.

That is why Uber papers are useful even when the substantive target is not gig work: they reveal what labor supply looks like when the schedule menu is unusually dense.
