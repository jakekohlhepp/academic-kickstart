Public finance and optimal taxation

Papers that translate labor-supply and taxable-income elasticities into welfare, tax formulas, and applied policy calibration.

The public-finance use case

Public finance often needs a tractable elasticity for welfare calculations or optimal tax formulas. The hard part is knowing which empirical object is legitimate for that use.

Reading path

Using Elasticities to Derive Optimal Income Tax Rates is the formula anchor. Optimal Redistributive Taxation with Both Extensive and Intensive Responses adds heterogeneity and margins. Review of Estimates of the Frisch Elasticity of Labor Supply is a practical calibration reference.

Link to ETI

The taxable-income literature is nearby because ETI can be a sufficient statistic under some conditions. The conditions matter.
